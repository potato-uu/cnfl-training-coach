# PART 1 — Garage Strength / Dane Miller

## 1. Football Strength Structure (Annual Periodization — Parabolic Model)

Garage Strength uses a **5-phase Parabolic Periodization** across 18–24 weeks.

### Phase 1 — Exposure (GPP Foundation, Jan–Mar)
- High volume, moderate intensity; bodybuilding-style reacclimation
- Rep ranges: 7–15 per set; extended rest periods
- Benchmark lifts established: power snatch, power clean, back squat, bench press, pull-ups
- GPP block: 2–3 weeks; full adaptation within 5–6 weeks
- Goal: joint strengthening, hypertrophy base, technical movement foundation

### Phase 2 — Comprehension (Technical Development, Apr–Jun)
- Volume high-moderate; intensity climbs
- Olympic lift technique emphasized
- Complex exercises introduced for neural drive
- Intentional overreach — athletes may experience burnout (desired)
- Rep ranges: 4–6 per set

### Phase 3 — Ascension (Power, Jun–Aug)
- Volume decreases; intensity increases significantly
- Lower rep schemes with higher neurological intent
- Combine prep included (L-drill, shuttle, vertical/broad jump technique)
- "Exuberant athletes" (fast-twitch dominant) thrive here

### Phase 4 — Summit (3–5 weeks pre-season)
- 33% volume reduction (deload) in opening week
- 90%+ intensity on non-athlete days; heavy, complex lifts only
- 2–3 movements per session maximum

### Phase 5 — Realization (10–15 days pre-competition)
- Volume drops drastically; high recovery priority
- Simple, familiar exercises only
- Cue: "Lower the reps, lower the weight, move FAST AF"

### In-Season (Sep–Nov)
- 1–2 weeks reduced/no weightroom during preseason camp
- Dynamic adjustments based on opponent difficulty
- Recovery is primary priority

### Post-Season December
- 30 minutes twice/week; Olympic lifts at conversational intensity; nervous system recovery

**Block length:** 8–12 week blocks for planning; adjust based on athlete response.

## 2. Unilateral Training System

Unilateral training is the **dominant mode for skill position athletes** — not accessory, but primary.

**Rationale:** Football is played in unilateral positions. Bilateral strength without unilateral transfer is incomplete. Unilateral work exposes asymmetries that injury-proof athletes.

### Single-Leg Squat (SLS) — Rated #1
- "The BEST lift we have seen that correlates to speed" — enhances ground reaction force utilization
- Loading: 70% weight on front leg, 30% rear; back knee drives down; front knee tracks forward
- **Off-season prescription:** 5 sets × 7 reps/side (barbell)
- **Speed prescription:** 5–6 sets × 3–4 reps/side (barbell, maximum intent)
- Superset with Rotating Back Extension: 5 sets × 7 reps/side, FAST with intent

### Bulgarian Split Squat
- Setup: rear leg on single-leg roller/balance pad; front foot 4–6 inches from pad
- Eccentric: controlled descent, gently tap back knee to pad
- Volume: minimum 5 sets per session
- Modes: heavy barbell (strength phase) vs. bodyweight/dumbbell (hypertrophy)
- "Arguably the best exercise to transfer to a plethora of sports"; targets weak posterior chains

### Drop Cossack Squat
- Load: dumbbell or kettlebell
- Volume: 5–6 sets × 3–4 reps/side
- Function: lateral deceleration and force absorption for cutting
- Directly trains load-into-cut at speed
- Progression: traditional cossack → drop variation

### Barbell Step-Up
- Addresses structural imbalances; improves trunk control during acceleration phase
- One of the five primary speed-building lifts

### Good Morning Step-Up + Elevated Step-Up (Reflexive Strength Series)
- Volume: 4–5 sets × 2–3 reps/side
- Develops stumble reflex and crossed extensor reflex for top-end speed
- Third movement: Dumbbell Snatch to Box (same volume)

## 3. Position-Specific Training (WR/DB/RB vs. OL/DL)

### Skill Positions (WR, DB, RB) — Three Quality Priorities
1. **Absolute Strength** — overcome static starts, establish positioning vs. defenders
2. **Reactive Strength** — rapid cut entry, deceleration at speed, explosive re-acceleration
3. **Dynamic Trunk Control** — forward/backward lean, rotational control while jumping/receiving

**WR Weekly Structure:**
- Technical coordination: 2×/week
- Unilateral absolute strength: 1×/week
- Dynamic training: 1×/week
- Dynamic trunk control: 2×/week
- Plyometrics: minimum 1×/week

**WR Key Exercises:** Single-leg squats, drop cossack squats, rotational box jumps (bilateral + unilateral), hill sprints (10–30m), heavy sled pulls (5–6 × 20m), power cleans

**RB Specifics:**
- 90–95% of RB game play occurs in acceleration phase, not max velocity
- Key: snatches/cleans/front squats/single-leg squats for DTC; back squats/step-ups/heavy sleds for max strength; banded skater squats; hill sprints with cuts

**DB/WR Rotational Symmetry:**
- Rotational plyos performed equally BOTH directions
- "Reckless jumps" (depth drop → hop → jump → mid-air rotation → broad jump) develop multi-directional explosive capacity

### OL/DL — Four Quality Priorities
1. **Sustained Impulse** (~0.4 seconds from snap) — 12–15 singles of hang power cleans; 2 box power cleans/min → 1/min
2. **Absolute Strength** — full range unilateral: walking lunges, cossack, overhead squats, bench, back/front squats
3. **Dynamic Trunk Control** — rotational capacity; front squats paired with torso rotations
4. **Impulse Endurance** — OTM power cleans at 70%

**OL 4 Key Lifts:** Mid Butt Snatch (from floor, 3-point stance simulation); Angled Football Bar Bench Press (pass protection lockout); Side Med Ball Throws (rotational trunk power); Single Leg Squat (hip mobility + unilateral)

**Volume by Position (Film-Analyzed):**
- OL: 50–70 plays/game, 7–12 yards/play → more absolute strength, heavier loads, mass priority
- WR: 50–70 plays/game, 10–18 yards/play → more reactive strength, plyometric volume, lateral/rotational work
- Do NOT run WRs and OL through the same conditioning protocol

## 4. Posterior Chain Development

### Exercise Hierarchy for Football
1. **Power Clean / Low Hang Clean** — primary posterior chain driver; "lights up the posterior chain and targets all muscle groups"; off-season: 4×2 → 2×4
2. **Single-Leg Squat** — unilateral posterior chain + quad integration; #1 speed correlate
3. **Back Extension (Rotating)** — superset with SLS; 5 sets × 7 reps/side, FAST; targets erectors and glutes
4. **GHR / Glute-Ham Raise** — isometric holds followed by 15 traditional reps; coordinated hamstring/trunk engagement
5. **Barbell Step-Up** — structural imbalances; sprint mechanics
6. **Hamstring Curls** — 3–4 sets × 10–20 reps; slow eccentric emphasis; hamstrings respond to tempo
7. **Back Extensions (standard)** — 6–8 reps loaded to 40 reps volume
8. **Seated Good Mornings** — 40–50 reps targeting low back + adductors
9. **Dumbbell Walking Lunges** — 4–5 steps/set; glute/hamstring + cardio stimulus

**Key principle:** "Glutes and hamstrings sweep together like a well-formed puzzle." Posterior chain strength directly drives acceleration from drive phase.

## 5. Olympic Lift Integration

**Core to Garage Strength football — not supplementary.**

- Olympic lifts open every session (power before strength, strength before accessory)
- Off-season: technique foundation (Phase 1–2); power expression peaks (Phase 3)
- In-season OL: OTM power cleans at 70% max; 12–15 singles → 2 reps/min → 1 rep/min

**Position-specific:**
- Skill positions: hang variations; emphasis on bar speed and neural drive
- OL: floor-based variations (mid-butt snatch, box cleans); emphasis on sustained impulse

**Why it works for football:**
- "Slow eccentric past the knees and tight back" on low hang clean = posterior chain pattern identical to burst off the line
- Mid Butt Snatch from floor mimics coming off snap from 3-point stance; develops "crack shoulders" explosiveness
- Clean teaches force absorption (eccentric) AND force expression (concentric) — both critical for football

## 6. Plyometric System for Football

**Governing Principle:** "Force over time" (impulse), not peak force. Performed minimum 1×/week; rotational and lateral work is non-negotiable for skill positions.

### Exercise Library

**Bilateral Foundation:**
- Skips for Distance: 20-yard benchmark, 4–5 sets; track skip count reduction
- Hurdle Hop to Double Leg Bound: 10-yard distances; elite athletes clear in 3 bounds
- Hydro Bag Switches: 10–12 reps through 5 mini-hurdles; 10° foot angle; upright posture
- Single Leg Bounds: 30-yard distances, alternating; track stride count reduction

**Rotational (Skill Position Priority):**
- Depth Drop → 90° Rotational Box Jump: both directions equally
- Gwiz Jumps: knee-based single-leg rotation jumps landing on boxes (RB-specific)
- "Reckless Jumps": depth drop → forward hop → jump → mid-air rotation → land → broad jump; superset with single-leg stair jumps

**Unilateral / Complex:**
- Hurdle Series: 3 small hurdles single-leg → 2 high hurdles bilateral → opposite single-leg → small hurdle → low box jump; transfers to "jump cuts and sudden stops"
- Single-Leg Stair Jumps: drive phase + acceleration; "knee up, toe up" (dorsiflexion cue); start with "bad leg" first

### Contrast Training
Back Squat (5×3, 2/1/x tempo) superset with Double Broad Jump (5×1) — single highest-transfer gym-to-field protocol.

### Sample Session Structures

**Off-Season Skill Position Day:**
1. Low Hang Clean — 4×2 → 2×4
2. Incline Bench Press — 5×5 (1-and-¼ reps)
3. Single Leg Squat — 5×7/side (barbell) superset with Rotating Back Extension — 5×7/side FAST
4. Towel Grip Pull-Up — 4×12 superset with Miracle Grow — 4×12–15

**Speed Development Day:**
1. Power Clean or Olympic variation (opener)
2. Forward Sled Pull — 5–6×20m (heavy once weekly; lighter mid-week)
3. Single-Leg Squat — 5–6×3–4/leg (barbell)
4. Drop Cossack Squat — 5–6×3–4/side (kettlebell)
5. Hill Sprints — 10–30m (position-specific volume)

# PART 2 — Overtime Athletes

## 1. Field Transfer Principles (Gym → Field)

### The Core Argument
Gym strength does NOT automatically transfer to field agility. The missing links:
1. Ankle function (inversion/eversion capacity)
2. Hip function (abduction/adduction stability)
3. Deceleration capacity (eccentric + isometric absorption)

The transfer chain: ankle mobility/stability → hip stability → eccentric deceleration → reactive COD → football-speed cuts. Each step must be established before the next.

### Highest Transfer Training Methods
- Single-leg broad jumps + single-leg broad-to-90° jumps: directly mirror force angles of plant-and-cut
- Contrast training (heavy squat superset with broad jumps): CNS priming + explosive expression
- Lateral half-kneeling starts: trains hip-loading position of lateral cut initiation
- DB elevated reverse lunge: "carries over to the field almost seamlessly" — mirrors acceleration-step position
- Ground contact time reduction across all pogo and bound variants

### Lab/Gym Metrics That Predict Field COD
- Ground contact time during pogo variations (shorter = better reactive strength)
- Single-leg landing stability (immediate stabilization without wobble post-landing)
- Lateral ankle control during lateral line hops
- Eccentric deceleration quality: quiet landings, knees tracking over toes, immediate positional ownership

**Critical warning:** Athletes who only lift heavy and skip the ankle/hip foundation + plyometric bridge remain at same field speed despite gym gains.

## 2. COD Drill Library & Progressions (Complete, With Entry Criteria)

### Phase 1 — Ankle/Hip Foundation (Entry: Basic bilateral jumping)

**Lateral Line Hops**
- Feet together, hop laterally over line, no forward/backward drift
- 10 seconds continuous, count touches; bilateral → single leg → increased distance
- Entry criteria: basic bilateral ankle stability

**Lateral Cone Hops**
- 4 cones 12 inches apart; "land with big toe in line with top of each cone," pause 1 second at end
- Progression: eliminate pause (reduces amortization phase)
- Entry criteria: proficiency with lateral line hops

### Phase 2 — Deceleration Control (Entry: Stable bilateral landing)

**Multi-Step Decel, No Complete Stop**
- Sprint 10 yards, decelerate over 3–5 steps without stopping
- Cue: "land fast, stop hard" — allow drift initially, tighten over sessions

**Multi-Step Decel, Complete Stop**
- Sprint → decelerate → complete stop, hold 3 seconds
- Cue: "Own the bottom position. No post-landing adjustments."
- Performance marker: quiet landings, strong posture, knees tracking clean, hips loaded

**Single Leg Dead Stop**
- Sprint → plant single leg → hold 3 seconds
- "Maximum stress on single leg — most challenging of the three"
- Entry criteria: full control on both multi-step variations

### Phase 3 — COD Technique (Entry: Phase 2 complete)

**Lateral Shuffle Shuttle**
- 3 cones, 2.5 yards apart; push off rear leg to far cone → foot chop → drive rear leg back
- Cue: "Push the ground away with your back leg, not just reaching"
- Entry criteria: basic lateral shuffling

**Basic Y-Cut**
- Y-shaped cone layout, 5 yards between; coach gives directional cue at top cone
- Volume: ~10 sets × 1 rep, split evenly L/R; start slow-paced, advance to sprint entry speed
- Entry criteria: Phase 2 complete

**Aggressive Y-Cut** → entry criteria: consistent basic Y-cut
**Full 90-Degree Cut** → entry criteria: aggressive Y-cut mastery
**Basic 180-Degree Turn** → entry criteria: 90° cut mastery
**Full 180-Degree Turn** → entry criteria: basic 180° drill
**Curvilinear Series:** Quarter arcs → Half figure-8s → Full figure-8s → Serpentine runs (mimics route stems)

### Phase 4 — Reactive / Sport-Speed COD (Entry: All Phase 3 mastered)

**Single Leg Broad to 90° Jump**
- 4 cones in T-shape; single-leg broad jump → immediately push laterally → bilateral landing
- Entry criteria: lateral broad jump + single-leg stability + basic reactive movement

**Head-to-Head Competition Format**
- Two athletes synchronized (skater hops → 5-yard sprints)
- Adds reactive/unpredictable element; simulates WR-DB live coverage

**Note:** 5-10-5 and L-Drill are TEST tools, not training tools. Use Phases 1–4 to build; use 5-10-5 to verify.

## 3. Deceleration System

### Biomechanics
"Most non-contact injuries happen during deceleration, not acceleration." Injuries occur at plant, lateral cut, landing, braking — not push-off. Two force absorption mechanisms:
1. **Eccentric control** — absorb force smoothly through full ROM
2. **Isometric absorption** — completely stop momentum and stabilize instantly

Strong in gym but lacking isometric absorption = cannot execute sharp cuts (deceleration consumes too long a time window before re-acceleration begins).

### Deceleration Exercise Progressions

| Exercise | Mechanism | Key Cue |
|---|---|---|
| Sprinter Step-Ups | Eccentric unilateral control | "Control descent; don't shift weight to trailing leg" |
| Single-Leg Broad Jump to Landing | Horizontal eccentric absorption | "Own the landing. No post-contact adjustments." |
| Russian Lunges | Deep-position stability + hip transition | Rapid hip loading on descent |
| Speed Russian Lunges | Reactive version | Minimize time in bottom |
| Approach Tuck Jumps to Split Lunge | Reactive landing under height + speed | "Control on impact" |

### Quantifying Improvement
- Landing noise reduction (quieter = better eccentric control)
- Elimination of post-landing adjustments
- Faster re-acceleration after stop
- Sharper cut angle at same speed
- Ground contact time reduction on pogos

### The Rule
"Land fast, stop hard, stabilize instantly" — zero wobble, quiet landing, immediate postural control.

## 4. WR/DB Football-Specific Movement

### WR Archetype Framework

**Possession Receiver (X/Z):** Strength-based explosive movements; route stem precision; break-out-of-cut force; "powerful complex movements that force them to transfer energy through their core efficiently"

**Speed/Burner Receiver (Z):** Linear speed primary; form running, stride mechanics, vertical jump; acceleration and top-end velocity dominate

**Slot/YAC Receiver (H/Slot):** Reactive agility, not just planned COD; "everything they do is to react sharply off defenders"; needs highest reactive COD training volume

### Universal WR Non-Negotiables
1. Complex explosive movements (ball-release and break execution)
2. Running efficiency (biomechanically sound mechanics)
3. Agility (cutting precision from core stability — not just foot speed)
4. Craft refinement (eyes, reaction, hands, route mastery)

### DB/Cornerback Specificity
- Technique over raw speed: "Many corners without elite 40 times put clamps on guys running 4.38s"
- Athletic stance: feet outside hip-width for faster lateral initiation
- Core stability is foundation of agility
- Mirror drill (both athletes face each other, synchronized reactive movement) = primary training tool
- Reactive COD drills (coach-directed Y-cuts, head-to-head) are DB primary training

### Route-to-Drill Mapping
| Route Type | Best Drill |
|---|---|
| Crossing / slant | Multi-step decel, no stop |
| Comeback / square-in | Multi-step decel, complete stop |
| Post / corner / out | Single-leg dead stop |
| All breaks | Y-cut progression (slow → sprint speed) |
| Comeback / hitch-and-go | 180° turn series |
| Wheel / option / reactive reads | Curvilinear series (arcs, figure-8s, serpentines) |
| YAC (slot) | Phase 4 reactive drills |

## 5. Ankle/Hip Foundation (Complete Progressions)

### Ankle Mobility

**Kneeling Banded Ankle Stretch**
- Thick band anchored low, half-kneeling; drive knee forward over toe, heel flat
- Duration: 2 minutes/side; progression: add kettlebell on knee

**Standing Weighted Single-Leg Calf Stretch**
- Hold dumbbell same side, keep leg straight, sink into heel
- Duration: 2 minutes/side; targets gastroc + soleus under load

**Heavy Sled Push**
- Bridges mobility to performance: drive through balls of feet, full dorsiflexion/plantarflexion range, long powerful strides
- "Range without strength is unstable" — sled work forces production through new ankle ranges

### Ankle Strength Progressions
All sessions begin with pogo warmup sequence:
- Forward pogos 2×10 yards
- Alternating (single-leg) pogos 2×10 yards
- Lateral pogos 2×10 yards each direction
- Lateral single-leg pogos
- Lateral line hops (10 seconds, count touches)
- Pulsing pogos (minimal amplitude, maximal frequency)

### Hip Stability Progression
**Banded March (Anti-Rotation):** Band around shoulder, partner holds; march backward fighting rotation
**Lateral Half-Kneeling Starts:** Explosive lateral movement from loaded position; trains hip abductor-driven lateral COD
**DB Elevated Reverse Lunge:** 3×8/leg; extended range, hip flexor/extensor emphasis; "carries over to field almost seamlessly"
**Bosu Ball Reverse Lunge:** Unstable surface; active hip stabilization

### Ankle-Hip Integration Gate
If athlete cannot hold single-leg lateral line hops with knee tracking over toe for 10 seconds → NOT ready for Phase 2 COD drills, regardless of squat numbers.

## 6. Plyometric System for Football COD

**Rule:** "Plyometrics are high-intensity neural exercises demanding precision — never during fatigue. Always in first 20 minutes of a session."

### Level 1 — Ankle-Dominant
Forward pogos, alternating pogos, lateral pogos, lateral single-leg pogos, lateral line hops, pulsing pogos

### Level 2 — Hip-Knee Integration
- Squat jumps: 3×12, 30s rest
- Box jumps: 3×6–10, 30s rest; cue: "bend knees, swing arms, land softly"
- Skater jumps: 3×20 seconds, 15s rest; cue: "slight knee bend, immediate redirect"
- Lateral power shuffle: 3×6 bounds/side or 10–20 yards, 30s rest

### Level 3 — Single-Leg Power
- Single-leg double broad jumps (load hip → explode → land same leg → second jump → stick)
- Sprinter step-ups: 3×8/leg (explosive up, controlled down, no weight shift)
- Alternating bounds for height (skip-rhythm, aggressive knee drive)
- Band-resisted single-leg bounds (hip extension against resistance)

### Level 4 — Directional/Reactive
- Single-leg broad to 90° lateral jump: 4×1/leg
- Lateral to forward broad: 4×1/leg
- Jump cut: 4×1/leg (horizontal → lateral → forward single-leg landing)
- Ascending V-jumps: 4×1 (progressive cone spacing)

### Level 5 — Contrast / Complex (Experienced athletes only)
- Trap Bar Deadlift Jumps (5×3) superset with Triple Broad Jumps (5×1)
- Broad box jump → broad to vertical box jump → lateral broad to vertical box jump

**Volume Caps:** Level 4–5 drills: 30 contacts/session max. Stop when mechanics degrade — never condition with plyometrics.

## 7. Programming COD in Training Week

| Day | Focus | Key Elements |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 | Lower Strength + Plyo Base | Back squat 5×3, DB RDL 3×12, Double broad jump contrast, Pogo warmup |
| Day 2 | Speed / Acceleration | Pogo warmup, Half-kneeling starts, Wall drills, Lateral half-kneeling starts |
| Day 3 | Recovery / Mobility | Banded ankle stretch, sled walk, core stability |
| Day 4 | COD + Single-Leg Plyos | Phase 2–3 decel drills, Y-cut progression, Single-leg broad to 90°, Lateral shuffle shuttle |
| Day 5 | Reactive / Sport-Specific | Head-to-head drills, Curvilinear series, 180° turns, Sport simulation |

**Volume Caps:**
- Sprint volume (COD day): <300 yards in-season; up to 500 yards off-season
- Level 4–5 plyometric contacts: 30/session max
- COD drills: 10 sets/drill maximum; quality governs — stop when mechanics degrade

**Warmup (Every Session):**
1. Pogo variations (2 sets each, 10–15 yards)
2. Lateral power steps 2×15 yards/side
3. Carioca quick step 2×10 yards/direction
4. Crossover run 2×10 yards/direction
5. Lateral shuffle to sprint 2×10 yards

## 8. Assessment & Progress Markers

**Baseline Assessment Battery:**
- Lateral line hops: count touches in 10 seconds (bilateral → unilateral)
- Single-leg landing hold: 3 seconds without adjustment (pass/fail)
- Multi-step deceleration: quiet landing, knees over toes, no wobble (pass/fail)
- Y-cut time from sprint initiation through cut to next cone
- 5-10-5 Pro Agility: only after COD training established

**Red Flags → Regress One Phase:**
- Valgus collapse on any single-leg landing
- Audible landing (loud = eccentric deficit)
- Ankle roll during lateral hops
- Unable to hold single-leg dead stop 3 seconds after 3 sessions

**Testing Frequency:**
- Ground contact quality: each session (observational)
- COD drill speed: every 2 weeks
- 5-10-5 (formal timed): monthly
- Single-leg stability: weekly pass/fail
